The Struggle of Content Exploration

How can we make finding content to watch efficient and easy?

The days of scrolling through TV channels and trying to find a show or movie to watch on the couch is now over, but instead it is replaced by going through different entertainment applications and streaming services. The main problem with this new form of entertainment is shuffling through different applications to try and find what you want to watch, whether it’s keeping track of where you left off or trying to find a new show.

Me and my team created an app that gives users that struggle to find content to watch an organizational hub that unites multiple streaming services for effortless viewing. These users no longer have to waste countless minutes from shuffling between apps just to find the perfect content to watch.

My Role: Team Leader

Approaching the Problem

What did I do?

My role in my team was Project Manager/User Researcher/Prototyping. I became in charge of all the meetings, finalizing ideas, and making sure we hit all the criteria and deadlines. I organized meeting times that worked for everyone, and each meeting were progressive steps on the project including research, user personas, storyboarding, etc.. I made sure I asked for everyone's input so no one felt like they weren't being heard while also having them provide different perspectives to help us narrow down exactly what we were looking for.

Research Methods

While thinking of how the app is going to be designed is important, approaching the problem and understanding user needs and struggles comes first. I gave myself and my team four different focuses to research on: Types of streaming services, feedback from streaming users who use paid subscriptions, who the target aduience is, and what problems people faced using streaming services. The type of research done was doing competitive analyses, sorting through relevant articles, and finding user feedback through media platforms such as reddit. With all the research done I was able to get a better idea of who our target audience is and came up with a couple of user personas that fit the description.

Obstacles Faced and Design Process

What issues were found?

From our user personas and extensive research we found our demographic to be very diverse and didn’t know how to dive into a specific route. We did competitive research and analysis with other streaming services, but this hindered our ability to gain inspiration for how the application can be laid out since it was a new idea at the time with no other similar apps to reference to.

How did I face these issues?

With the demographic/scope being so trivial the best way to go about this was to generate problem statements, and come up with solutions. Me and my team came up with 4 problem statements, and came up with as many solutions we could think of for each. With the solutions we came up with I chose the 6 best. With that we then turned them into 6 different storyboards that gave a better idea of how the app became useful to users who watch content.

Bringing it All Together

Lo-Fi and Interviews

  • As a team we collectively came up with different sketches in order to feed ideas into each others brains and how the user flow could be mapped out
  • After creating a lo-fi prototype we conducted interviews with participants of all different demographics that at the same time fit our persona descriptions
  • Through our interviews and extensive research we were able to take the insight and put it into the hi-fi prototype and everything started to fall into place smoothly
  • I made the team create a moodboard to get a theme going for the app design and then from there came up with a final style guide

    Sort

    Login Screen

    The login screen is the first thing you see as a user, so giving a unique touch to it makes the user feel welcomed and familiar with the brand

    Connecting Accounts

    In order to make this an organizational hub the user must connect their accounts from accepted platforms. When leaving Sort to go connect to another application the user is given a screen that familiarizes themselves with the brand, just like the login screen.

    TV-Shows & Movies

    The first tab on the bottom bar is for TVs and movies. From here the user is suggested most recently watched and then their watchlist, and the user is able to click on the desired show or movie and can see all the previews.

    Livestreaming

    The second tab on the bottom bar leads to the list of livestreams the user follows. They are first suggested live channels that they can browse horizontally. If they want to see previous VODs the streamer has uploaded they just click on the channel and a list is given to them.

    Subscriptions

    The third tab on the bottom bar leads to the users subscription details. This tab allows the users to see their subscription analytics regarding their screen time and how much they've been paying. This can be seen for all subcription services and subscribed channels on Twitch/Youtube.

    Profile Options

    Additionally users are given the option to customize their profile, and they also have a filter option to help reorganize their app to their needs.

    Consideration and Growth Opportunities

    Sort has potential to be useful for not just users that need content to watch, but even the average consumer. There are still many considerations to take into account such as giving more ogranization options, making the app more familiar to pre-existing streaming services, and fixing up the font/style-guide to be a bit more clean and modern. Future steps that can be taken is to implement more user research and get feedback to further improve the app.
