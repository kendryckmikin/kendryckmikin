Side-By-Side

Redesign of Tik-Tok's filming and editing process making

Start Design Journey

Tiktok content creators need an easy way to cut and refilm clips in order to speed up their editing process and release more content on time

Tik-Tok is a growing app that has over 1.6 billion users on it as of 2023, so giving the ability to create quality content on Tik-Tok is very important for their content creators that have to entertain the vast amount of users.
The issue is that Tik-Tok content creators need a more efficient way to cut, refilm, and edit their content in order to make the entire filming and editing process easy and intuitive.

My Role

I was chosen as the team leader for this project and with the role as team leader I set up all the meetings, communicated back and forth with the professor and his input, made sure everyone’s insight was taken into account and made final decisions.

Constraints

The prompt that was addressed to us was to come up with an innovative idea that makes production efficient. This can be for any application as long as it's related to production.

Motivation

Our initial motivation was diving into short videos’ design/creative process as it’s popularity is going to only increase from now. So our biggest challenge was trying to figure out how we could make the entire creative process easier and more efficient for users that make content catered for short videos

Narrowing Down

We went over 24 total ideas with their own sketches relating to mobile editing in general and then narrowed it down to short videos.

With the narrowed down versions we asked for input from our peers on which ideas were the best and we came to the idea of side-by-side and markers for Tik-Tok.
From there we spent hours just debating as a team on the amount of different factors that could be problematic trying to add our features into Tik-Tok and came up with lo-fi prototypes to showcase to our peers for even more feedback.

Ideation and Lo-Fi

From our peers, the professor, and group debates we found out the strengths and weaknesses of the features we wanted to add and how it could be imported smoothly. We conducted a lot of competitive analyses with Tik-Tok's filming and editing along with other mobile editing apps discussing what they did right and wrong. Taking into account all of the research and feedback we recieved we implemented it all into our Lo-Fi designs. With these Lo-Fi designs we were able to receive more feedback from our peers making the path to the final product very clear.

After the countless hours of debates and trying to come to final decisions as a team we were able to come up with our final prototype after taking into account our peers insight and how it could be designed to be useful for tik-tok content creators.

The Final Product

Introducing Side-By-Side

Adding new features to Tik-Tok's filming and editing process, our redesign garners methods of efficiency.
The main features include: Sound Markers, a retake option, a library, and allowing side-by-side comparisons with clips.

Backgroun and Hi-Fi Prototype

Have you ever seen a Tik-Tok trend that you thought looked fun and easy to make in a few minutes? Then when you actually try to get into the process of filming and editing the Tik-Tok you end up staying there for way longer than you expected, just trying to get the perfect take and attempting to precisely line up transition cuts. Then with the filming process done, you’re there for even longer trying to edit the Tik-Tok and end up unsatisfied with a few clips so you’d have to restart the filming process again. This becomes a burden as a content creator because of the amount of time already spent on the Tik-Tok goes down the drain.

Side-by-side was designed with the intention of making these inconveniences

bearable, and in hopes of speeding up the whole process with efficiency. For assistance with recording Side-by-side added sound markers that the user can place for visual aid when filming for perfect cut transitions, and a retake button was added as an option in case the user finds a take to be useful but not the best. With the actual Side-by-side feature when users enter the editing process they are presented with a library with all the accumulated clips that can be used to compare two similar clips “side-by-side.”
