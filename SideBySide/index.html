Side-By-Side

Redesign of Tik-Tok's filming and editing process making

Start Design Journey

Why does Tik-Tok need a redesign?

Have you ever seen a Tik-Tok trend that you thought looked fun and easy to make in a few minutes? Then when you actually try to get into the process of filming and editing the Tik-Tok you end up staying there for way longer than you expected, just trying to get the perfect take and attempting to precisely line up transition cuts. Then with the filming process done, you’re there for even longer trying to edit the Tik-Tok and end up unsatisfied with a few clips so you’d have to restart the filming process again. This becomes a burden as a content creator because of the amount of time already spent on the Tik-Tok goes down the drain. Tik-Tok is a growing app that has over 1.6 billion users on it as of 2023, so giving the ability to create quality content on Tik-Tok is very important for their content creators that have to entertain the vast amount of users.

The issue is that Tik-Tok content creators need a more efficient way to cut, refilm, and edit their content in order to make the entire filming and editing process easy and intuitive.

Introducing Side-By-Side

Adding new features to Tik-Tok's filming and editing process, this redesign garners methods of efficiency.
The main features include: Sound Markers, a retake option, a library, and allowing side-by-side comparisons with clips.

Final Product

With Side-by-side I wanted to implement new and innovative ways that makes filming and editing for Tik-Tok content creators as efficient as possible. The features that are introduced doesn't get rid of anything, but instead adds more shortcuts and beneficial qualities. We'll go through all of the features in action with explanations.

Filming:

1. Adding Sound Markers

  • Sound markers are used for visual aid when recording
  • Found in sounds for convenience
  • When marker is clicked you go into marker mode
  • Drag the marker onto desired timestamp
  • Clicking marker turns it red indicating the recording stops at that time

2. Recording With Sound Markers

  • Sound markers are used for visual aid when recording
  • When recording with a red sound marker, the recording stops when it reaches the marker

3. Retake Button

  • Let's say you like the first take, but you believe you can improve from it
  • You can use the retake button to film that part again and save previous clips
  • When the video is retaken, the buttone has a "+1" that adds sequentially after each take

4. Navigate to Editing

  • To keep the user from being confused to the new UI, I kept
    the process to navigate to editing the same

Editing:

1. Library

  • The original way to add clips and photos to a Tik-Tok was done by pressing a "+" that opens your photo library
  • Because Side-by-Side adds a retake option, multiple clips are added into a library
  • This library is accessed by pressing the edit button and bringing the tab up
  • Clips that are currently being used are highlighted
  • You're also given the option to import your own clips/photos

2. Comparing Clips

  • To compare two clips Side-by-Side just click a desired clip from the library
  • It's then put up next to the current clip in use
  • Sound markers are also visible on the timeline to help reference where transitions/cuts were made

3. Choosing the Best Clip

  • Once you decided which of the two clips is best you highlight the one you want to delete
  • Then when the clip is highlighted, pressing the bottom right "delete" button will get rid of the clip

Future Improvements

Side-by-Side takes Tik-Tok's editing and fimling process to a whole new level. Being given the option to have sound markers, perform retakes that adds into a library, and compare two clips Side-by-Side gives Tik-Tok content creators the ability to enhance their workflow.

While there's a lot of promise shown in this redesign there's still room for improvement. While discoverability was heavily considered in the design process I still believe there can be better solutions. Also all of the new features are brand new concepts to most users, and learning them can be overwhelming. So there can definitely be ways to work on the simplicity of the features that makes it easier to use and learn.
